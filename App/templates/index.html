Pre-Operative Evaluation

The pre-operative assessment is a crucial step in identifying co-morbidities that could lead to complications during anaesthesia, surgery, or the post-operative period. Patients scheduled for elective procedures typically undergo this assessment 2 to 4 weeks before their surgery. This article explores the key components of an effective pre-operative history, physical examination, and routine investigations that may be required.

Pre-Operative History

A pre-operative history follows the standard structure of medical history-taking but includes additional anaesthetic and surgery-specific considerations.

History of the Presenting Complaint

This section provides a brief overview of why the patient requires surgery and the specific procedure planned. If applicable, it is essential to confirm the surgical site to avoid errors. Certain conditions may be particularly relevant for the anaesthetist. For example, head and neck surgery may indicate abnormal airway anatomy, which could impact airway management.
